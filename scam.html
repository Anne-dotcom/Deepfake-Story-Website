The story of a man who was conned into thinking that his former colleague called him for money...


Turns out it was a DEEPFAKE!

Scroll down











The increasing amounts of news, talking about how people were scammed by deepfake technology is ever increasing. There are multiple money laundering scams taking place by manipulating and acting like loved ones or known people.

A 73 year-old man in India fell victim to a deepfake scam after receiving a call from someone impersonating his former colleague and asking for money.

KOCHI: It was around 5:45 am on July 9th that PS Radhakrishnan, a resident of Kozhikode in Kerala, first received a call from an anonymous number.

Few minutes later, after ignoring it, he receives a WhatsApp call. The man on the other end said that he was currently at the Dubai airport, waiting to board a flight to India. He had called to ask for a financial favor.



His sister-in-law was being prepped for an emergency surgery at a hospital in Mumbai, and a sum of ₹40,000 had to be paid urgently as an advance. He said the money had to be transferred via UPI to the phone of someone with her at the Mumbai hospital.

Even though the caller sounded like his former colleague, Radhakrishnan had his fears about online scams and the danger of transferring money to an unknown account. The man then immediately said that he would do a video-call.

Within a few seconds, he called and looked exactly like Radhakrishnan's former colleague. Only his face was visible. His lips and eyes moved like any normal person as we talked in English. The call lasted just 25 seconds. He later came back on a voice call and spoke about the urgency for money. Radhakrishnan didn’t ask further questions and transferred the money.

When the same person called back after just a few minutes and asked for more money to take care of hospital expenses, Radhakrishnan felt a a rising suspision for the first time. There was a hurried tone in his voice which made Radhakrishnan lie about not having enough money in his account.

Due to his suspicions Radhakrishnan called his former colleague on the number he had saved; the man said he had not called at all!

Radhakrishnan realised that he had been tricked by a sophisticated deepfake technology scam. On talking to his other colleagues, he discovered that this was not just an isolated incident.

The police moved fast. They tracked the money to an account in Maharashtra. "This is the first time we are dealing with cyberfrauds using deepfakes." said the police officer.
